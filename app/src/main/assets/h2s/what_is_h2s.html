What is H2S

One of the most common occupational health problems that occur at the work­sites including in geothermal and petroleum drilling sites is toxic gas. Identifying toxic gases is essential to take necessary precautions and steps to protect your life.

This manual only focuses on Hydrogen Sulfide as many fatality incidences involve this particular gas.

What is H2S?

Hydrogen Sulfide is a lethal gas, which is a natural by-product of organic decay. Its chemical formula is H2S and they are often referred to as sour gas, stink damp and rotten egg gas.

Where Do You Find H2S?

To predict possible locations, you need to familiarize yourself with nature of the gas. It may accumulate wherever a mixture of hydrocarbons and sulfur are found. Also, since it is a natural by-­product of organic decay, H2S can be found wherever organic decomposition occurs, such as at oil and gas drilling operations, production operations, plant operations, farms, sewage, pulp and paper factories, refineries, etc.

In drilling operations, the source of H2S is in the geological formation, although the gas may also be released from the drilling fluids at the well bore, around the Blow Out Prevented (BOP), shale shaker, degasser, choke manifold or from the drilling fluid's piping system.
