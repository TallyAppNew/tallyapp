Occupational Exposure Limits (OEL), Toxicity Levels and Scale

H2S is a very toxic and can affect your health ; if you are exposed to this gas your potential health problems can range from minor discomfort to death. Recognizing the characteristics of the gas and knowing where it can be found is not sufficient. You must also know your allowable exposure limits. Moreover, since potential locations of H2S are generally known, you should identify specifics locations the gas is released to the atmosphere and proceed to leave/avoid the area.


Occupational Exposure Limits (OEL)

Limits are set by governing bodies to provide information on the allowable exposure limits to toxic chemicals found in the work place.

The OEL or H2S are as follows :

  • 10 PPM Time Weighted Average (TWA), maximum for an eight hour day. Workers allowed 10 PPM TWA exposures to H2S for an 8 hour period, provided that they do not exceed the limits for 15 PPM in that 8 hour period.

  • 15 PPM Short Term Exposure for not more than 15 minutes, with not more than four exposures per 8 hour day. Each exposure must be separated by at least 60 minutes of no exposures.

  • 20 PPM Ceiling Exposure Limit means that workers should not be exposed to 20 PPM H2S at any time. This medicates that it is permissible to have 19 PPM exposure for a very short time and still be in line with all the OELs.

Toxicity Levels


  • 1 PPM = H2S odor is noticeable

  • 10 PPM = 8 hour OEL

  • 20 PPM = ceiling OEL

  • 100 PPM = loss of smell in 2 to 15 minutes; burning throat, headache and nausea

  • 200 PPM = rapid loss of smell; burning eyes and throat

  • 500 PPM = loss of reasoning and balance, respiratory failure in 2 to 15 minutes - prompt resuscitation needed.

  • 700 PPM = immediate unconsciousness, seizures, loss of control of bladder and bowel, respiratory failure, and inevitable death if not rescued promptly

  • 1000 PPM = immediate unconsciousness, permanent brain damage or inevitable death unless rescued promptly

Scale


  • PPM = Parts of gas Per Million parts of air by volume

  • % = Percentage of gas in the air. E.g.,10,000 PPM =1%

  • 1 PPM = 1110,000 of 1%

  • 10 PPM = 111,000 of 1%

  • 100 PPM =11100 of l%

  • 1,000 PPM = 1110 of 1%

  • 10,000 PPM = 1%

  • 100,000 PPM = 10%

  • Energy Resources Conservation Board (ERCB) uses moles/kmole measurement. E.g., 10 moles/kmole = 10,000 PPM = 1%

INFLUENCING FACTORS


There are several factors that trigger the level of reaction of the human body to H2S. In addition to HOW the poisonous gas enters the body, other factors include :

  • Amount of Toxic Substance

The amount of toxic substance entering the human body determines whether the substance

will cause harmful effect.

  • Speed of Blood Stream Absorption

The more rapid a substance is absorbed. The more harmful it may be to the body. In several

case, even a small amount of toxic substance can be harmful if rapidly absorbed by the blood

stream.

  • Concentration of Toxic Substance

A poisonous substance with low concentration requires tremendous amount of concentration

to cause poisoning. Poisonous substances with high concentration only require little

concentration to cause harmful effects.

  • Direct Exposure of Human Body

Human bodies react differently to toxic substance exposures. Some tend to experience short

period of irritation

  • Effectiveness of Human Antibody

Substance can enter into and be discharged from the human body without creating any harm.

The human antibody detoxifies substances and discharges them harmlessly, although some

discharge may be less toxic, or in some cases more toxic than when the substance enters the

body.
