Physical Properties of H2S

The characteristics of gas are usually found in Material Safety Data Sheet (MSDS)

Characteristics of H2S :

  • Color : colorless, invisible.

  • Odor : H2S has a distinctive offensive odor resembling "rotten eggs'. However, H2S impairs the sense of smell very rapidly, even at low concentrations.

  • Vapor density : H2S is heavier than air so it settles in low areas, seen as pits, cellars and tanks.

  • Explosive limits : H2S will explode when mixed with the right proportion of air or oxygen.

  • Flammability : H2S bums with a blue flame, producing SOS that irritates the eyes and lungs.

  • Solubility (in water) : The gas can be dissolved in liquids, which means that it can present in any container or vessel used to carry or hold well fluids, including oil, water, emulsion and sludge. The solubility of H2S is dependent on temperature and pressure. If the conditions are right, simply agitating a fluid containing H2S may release the gas into the air.
